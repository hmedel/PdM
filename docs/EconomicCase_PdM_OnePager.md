# PhAIMaT · Predictive Maintenance for Fleets

## The real return on maintenance isn't in the parts. It's in the crash that never happened.

Shop spend is barely **~9 % of cost per mile**. The real money is in the **expected cost of an on-road
failure** — dominated by a single event your cost sheet never accounts for.

---

### What a failure you didn't see coming actually costs

| | |
|---|---|
| 🚨 **$15.23 M** | Cost of a **fatal** large-truck crash (FMCSA) |
| ⚖️ **$36 M** | Median *nuclear verdict* against motor carriers (2022) |
| 🔧 **1 in 4** | Vehicles placed **out of service** for a defect at inspection (23.2 %) |
| 📉 **+65 %** | More crashes among carriers with a poor maintenance record |

> Against a $15M or $36M event, **any savings on parts is noise.**
> Predictive maintenance manages the **tail of the risk**, not the shop invoice.

---

### Why it works — and it's not a vendor's promise

The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE/FEMP) documents what a predictive program delivers:

**10× ROI · Breakdowns −70 to −75 % · Downtime −35 to −45 % · Maintenance cost −25 to −30 %**
— plus **+8 to 12 %** over a calendar-based preventive program.

The risk is measurable and preventable: **brakes and tires** are the top two causes of out-of-service
violations, and **~1 in 3** truck crashes is attributable to brake failures.

---

🌎 **Aging fleets make it urgent:** in markets like Mexico, the cargo fleet averages **~19.3 years**
(68.8 % over 10 years old) vs ~6 in the U.S. Older fleets fail more. The case doesn't weaken — it
accelerates.

### What PhAIMaT does

On top of your fleet's telemetry, PhAIMaT **estimates the remaining life** of every critical component
— brakes, tires, wheel-ends, aftertreatment — **computes the optimal intervention time** per component
and adjusts it to the asset's real condition, **prioritizing by tail cost** and **calibrating to your
own operating data.**
